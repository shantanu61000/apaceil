Conflicts of Interest

Employees have an obligation to the company to avoid business dealings that could lead to potential or actual conflicts of interest.

When does a conflict of interest arise?

An actual or potential conflict of interest arises when an employee is in a position which can influence a business decision for the company which may result in a monetary gain for the employee/ relative of an employee, or APAC interests are adversely effected.

What can be done in such cases?

In all such cases, the employee must make his/her interests known to APAC in writing and step away from the decision-making process.

Gift Policy

The objective of this policy is to maintain APAC’s reputation as a transparent, best in-class company, which acts with integrity and bases its decisions only on legitimate business considerations.

  • Receiving or giving gifts, inappropriate entertainment or any other perks from people with whom we do business is not acceptable, because doing so could imply an obligation or a conflict of interest.
  • The Company, regardless of the circumstances, does not permit the soliciting of gifts.
  • Unsolicited gifts which are not edible/ perishable items, should be returned to the donor with a thank you note quoting this policy.
  • Unsolicited gifts can be accepted only in case of edible/ perishable items up to a limit of Rs 1000 per gift. Such edible gifts should be shared with the team in office.
  • Any gift received or given with a value above Rs 1000 should be disclosed up-front to the Compliance Officer with reasons for the same and the action taken by the Employee.

Probation Period

Unless otherwise stated in the Letter of Appointment / Employment Letter, employees will work :

  • On probation basis for the first 90 calendar days (3 months) / or 180 days ( 6 months ) after their date of joining.
  • Any significant absence will automatically extend the probation period.
  • The probation period may be extended for a specific period. This extension could be upto a maximum period of 90 days beyond the standard probation period of 180 days.
  • While on probation an employees is not eligible for leave , unless in exceptional circumstances with approval of supervisor.
  • Any employee is eligible for consolidated leave from the date of joining but he / she can avail such leave only after confirmation

Medical Check-ups

  • APAC may initiate, prior to or during employment, medical checkups for employees to ascertain their good health and ability to carry out uninterrupted work with APAC.
  • The employee in such a case will need to submit himself/herself for the same to a doctor or clinic prescribed by APAC.

Hiring of Relatives

  • As a norm, relatives of employees will not be hired in APAC.
  • However, in exceptional circumstances, where specialized skills or separate businesses / locations are involved, this may be allowed subject to specific sanction of the CEO & the Group Chief People Officer.
  • The same policy is applicable to spouses of employees.

Transfer/Relocation

  • APAC reserves the right to relocate/ transfer employees to any place in India.
  • The transfer policy will apply to all official transfers.

EMPLOYEE LEAVES

Entitlement of leave in a Financial Year

  • 21 Working days per year of Consolidated leave.
  • Leave of Absence (LOA) is a period of time that the individual is granted permission to be away from his/her primary job, while maintaining the status of employee and being paid. This includes annual vacation, public holidays, and other type of leave days.
  • Leave means , authorized absence, and hence MUST be approved. Long Leave requires prior approval.

General Conditons

  1. Consolidated leave can be used for reasons of sickness of self and family, casual leave and annual leave for longer vacations.
  2. Leave can be availed in units of 1 day.
  3. An employee may be asked to submit a medical certificate in case of absences due to sickness of more than 2 days.
  4. Leave exceeding 3 days is required to be approved by the concerned approving authority well in advance i.e. a minimum of 7 days before the date of availing leave.
  5. It is encouraged that regional and HO teams use an annual leave planner to ensure smooth functioning of the office.
  6. The leave records will be maintained on a financial year basis i.e. from 1st April to 31st March.
  7. Management has the right to defer the leave period or refuse leave to anyone if the circumstances so require

General Conditons

  1. Any employee is eligible for consolidated leave from the date of joining but he / she can avail such leave only after confirmation. Any employee is eligible for consolidated leave from the date of joining but he / she can avail such leave only after confirmation
  2. Public / national holidays as declared by APAC at the beginning of each year and Sundays falling within the leave period shall not be counted as part of the leave.
  3. Employees are mandated to avail leave of a minimum of 10 calendar days in one block once in a financial year.
  4. All absences MUST be informed to the immediate supervisor and verbal approval taken, and regularized with a leave application on resuming work.

Carry Forward Leave Days

  1. Consolidated leave entitlement must be taken in the current year.
  2. In exceptional cases, especially where business reasons prevent an employee to use his / her leave days, a limited amount of 10 leave days may be carried over to the next financial year.
  3. The rest, unless availed in the year, shall lapse at the end of the financial year. The limit on carry forward will apply only after the first full year of employment.

Accumulation of Leave

  1. The maximum accumulation of consolidated Leave allowed is 10 days in addition to the current year's entitlement of 21 days.
  2. All leave accumulated over and above these days will lapse.

Public/National Holiday

  • Public / National holidays shall be announced at the start of each year.
  • There will be 12 declared holidays during the year over and above the consolidated leave.
  • Public / National holidays are in addition to the consolidated leave entitlement.

Annual Increments

Annual increments are based on performance of self and the performance of the company . Besides also depending on inflation & market benchmarks. Other details as follows :

  1. The cutoff date for annual increments will be September 30th. This means that an employee should have completed a minimum of 6 months service by March 31st before becoming eligible for increments in April of that year.
  2. If the employee has joined during an assessment period after the cut-off date of the assessment cycle he / she would be eligible for annual increments only in April of the next year; however, the annual increment would include the amount for the number of days that the employee was present during the previous assessment period as well.
  3. If an employee has joined before the cut off date but has not completed a full year within the Company by March 31st, then his/here increment will be suitably pro-rated based on the number of days worked.
  4. Maternity Leave cases: ML is treated as “with pay and statutory leave” hence, all ML days are counted for the annual increments. The evaluation rating for the period where the employee was on duty and working will be taken for the whole year in such cases.
  5. Leave Without Pay (LWP) cases: For LWP cases of more than 15 days, the increment will be suitably prorated.
  6. Misconduct: Anybody who has been involved in malpractices, misconduct, and issues of ethics, integrity, honesty or confidentiality is excluded from any benefits under the Performance Assessment & Appraisal Policy

Disbursements of Annual Increments

  1. Increments will usually be given once a year w.e.f. the month of April, for the period April to March.
  2. Increments will be based on the annual performance rating of the employee for the year under assessment and other factors which the Management may deem relevant.
  3. The reporting supervisor/ HOD as of March 31st, will be responsible for the assessment of the employee for the year under review. However, if the employee has worked under another supervisor for a period equal to or more than 90 days, then the current supervisor/HOD will ensure inputs from the previous supervisor/HOD before appraising the employee.
  4. If the last working day of the employee comes before the completion of the assessment period then he/she will not be entitled for increment for that entire assessment period.
  5. If the employee resigns from the organization anytime up till the disbursement of the increment, then the employee will not be eligible for the increment.

Prevention of Sexual Harassment (POSH)

APAC will deal with any incident of Sexual Harassment strictly and swiftly.

Sexual harassment would mean and include any of the following: 

  1. Unwelcome sexual advances, requests or demand for sexual favors, either explicitly or implicitly, in return for employment, promotion, examination or evaluation of a person towards any company activity;
  2. Unwelcome sexual advances involving verbal, non-verbal, or physical conduct such as sexually colored remarks, jokes, letters, phone calls, e-mail, gestures, showing of pornography, lurid stares, physical contact or molestation, stalking, sounds, display of pictures, signs, verbal or non-verbal communication which offends the individual’s sensibilities and affects her/his performance
  3. Eve teasing, innuendos and taunts, physical confinement against one’s will and likely to intrude upon one’s privacy
  4. Act or conduct by a person in authority which creates an environment at the workplace which is hostile or intimidating to a person belonging to the other sex
  5. Conduct of such an act at the work place or outside in relation to a third-party contract employee during the course of employment; and
  6. Any unwelcome gesture by an employee having sexual overtones

“Employee” means any person on the rolls of the Company including those on deputation, contract, temporary or part time employees or those working as consultants.

Any grievance in this regards can be sent to posh@apacfin.com
